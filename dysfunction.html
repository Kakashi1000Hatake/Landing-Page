Shock Wave Therapy – Explained

Get answers to common questions about this safe and effective treatment.

What is shock wave therapy?

Shock wave therapy uses low-intensity sound waves to improve blood flow to the penile area, stimulating new blood vessel growth and helping to restore erectile function naturally.

How does it help?

By promoting neovascularization (the formation of new blood vessels), shock wave therapy enhances the natural process of achieving and maintaining an erection.

Is it painful?

No, the procedure is non-invasive and generally painless. Patients may feel a mild tingling sensation.

How many sessions are needed?

The number of sessions varies depending on individual needs, but a standard treatment course typically involves multiple sessions over a few weeks.

Who benefits the most?

Men with vasculogenic ED (erectile dysfunction caused by blood vessel issues) often see the most significant benefits.

Is it safe?

Yes, it is considered a safe and effective therapy with minimal side effects.

Can it be combined with other treatments?

Absolutely. Shock wave therapy can be an excellent standalone treatment or part of a comprehensive plan that includes medications or lifestyle changes.
