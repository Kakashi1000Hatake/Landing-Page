Understanding the Root Cause

Psychiatric (Psychological) Reasons

  • •

    Depression: lowers libido and disrupts normal erectile and ejaculatory function, treated with antidepressants and psychotherapy.

  • •

    Performance anxiety: causes erection difficulties; managed with CBT and relaxation techniques.

  • •

    Chronic stress: increases cortisol, suppressing testosterone and sexual desire; addressed with stress management and mindfulness therapy.

  • •

    Relationship conflicts: reduce intimacy; improved with couple’s therapy and counseling.

  • •

    Generalized anxiety disorders: impair arousal and satisfaction; treated with anti-anxiety medications and psychotherapy.

Endocrine (Hormonal) Reasons

  • •

    Low Testosterone: causes reduced libido and erections; treated with testosterone replacement therapy.

  • •

    Diabetes Mellitus: damages nerves and vessels; improved by strict blood sugar control and metabolic management.

  • •

    Hyperprolactinemia: lowers testosterone and desire; treated with dopamine agonists.

  • •

    Thyroid Disorders: disturb sexual function; managed with thyroid hormone correction.

  • •

    Adrenal Disorders: affect cortisol and androgens; corrected with hormone replacement or adrenal disease treatment.
